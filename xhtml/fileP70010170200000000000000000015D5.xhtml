Paradox of Thrift

This second example is about your decision to save. A paradox is a statement that seems to contradict itself, like “Saving your money might lower your savings.” If you save more from your income, your savings will increase and your spending will decrease. But if many people save more and spend less, businesses experience falling sales, cut back production, and lay off workers so that incomes fall. Paradoxically, the result may be less saving, because without employment income, people have to withdraw their savings from banks rather than increase them. Economists call this the paradox of thrift. Again, what is true for one is not necessarily true for all.

Note

“Thrift” means being very careful in managing your money.
