Do Market Economies Quickly Self-Adjust?

So which focus is “right?” Do markets coordinate smart individual choices to produce the miracle of the continuous, ever-changing production of the products and services we want (microeconomic focus), or do markets often fail and produce undesirable outcomes like unemployment, falling living standards, bankruptcies, financial bubbles, and deflation or inflation (macroeconomic focus)?

As you probably guessed, there is no single right answer to the question. Sometimes markets work well and quickly in coordinating individual choices, sometimes not. Economists call the periodic ups and downs of overall economic activity business cycles (a concept defined in Chapter 6).
