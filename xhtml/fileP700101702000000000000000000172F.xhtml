Government Choices

Of the many possible government choices, we will focus on just two: government buying products and services, and government taxes and transfer payments (such as Employment Assistance payments and Canada Pension Plan payments).

Government policy decisions to leave the economy alone or influence it — hands-off or hands-on — take two forms — fiscal policy and monetary policy.

Fiscal policy (discussed in Chapter 12) uses government purchases, taxes, and transfers to achieve the macroeconomic outcomes of steady growth, full employment, and stable prices.
