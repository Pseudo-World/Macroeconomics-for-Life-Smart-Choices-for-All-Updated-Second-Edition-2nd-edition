Final Products and Services

A final product or service is consumed directly by consumers. A loaf of bread you buy at the bakery, or your new Honda Civic, are final products. The flour that the bakery purchases as an input to make the bread or the steel Honda purchases to make the car are called intermediate products and are not included in nominal GDP. Why? The value of the intermediate products is already included in the value of the final products, so to add the value of the flour to the value of the bread would be double counting. This double-counting problem is important, and we will come back to it in section 6.2.
