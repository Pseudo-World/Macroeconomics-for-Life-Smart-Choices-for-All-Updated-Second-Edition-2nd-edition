Value Added Equals Inputs’ Incomes

The third payoff is important because it helps you think like a macroeconomist. Value added is the sum of all wages paid to workers, rent paid to landowners, and profits paid to business entrepreneurs. (Our simple example doesn’t have interest paid on capital.) Value added not only equals the value of final outputs, it also equals the value of all incomes earned by owners of inputs ($12), shown at the bottom of Figure 6.3.
