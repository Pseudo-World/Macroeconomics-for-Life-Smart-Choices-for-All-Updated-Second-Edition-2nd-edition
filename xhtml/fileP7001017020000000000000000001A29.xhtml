Income and Consumer Spending

On the right side, consumers earn income by selling inputs to businesses. The flow of physical inputs is left out, leaving only the dollar flow of income to consumers. Economists use the letter Y for income. On the left side, consumers use their income to buy products and services from businesses. The flow of physical products and services is left out, leaving only the dollar flow of consumer spending going to businesses. Economists use the letter C for consumption spending by consumers.
