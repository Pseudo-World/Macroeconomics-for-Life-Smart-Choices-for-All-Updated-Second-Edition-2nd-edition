Consumer Choices

Consumers can choose to spend their income or to save it. The green line with arrows pointing both ways between consumers and banks is the flow of consumer savings into, or loans out of, the banking system.

The income that consumers can spend or save is called disposable income — income after net taxes have been paid to government. Net taxes, represented by the letter T, equal taxes paid to government minus transfer payments (Employment Assistance, Old Age Security) consumers receive from government. The green line with arrows pointing both ways between consumers and government is the flow of net taxes paid by ­consumers to government.
