When Macroeconomic Dreams Come True: Potential GDP and Economic Growth

  1. 6.3 Explain how economic growth occurs and how it is measured.

Have you ever been lectured by a parent, teacher, or guidance counsellor about not living up to your potential? Haven’t we all? Well, if the economy always lived up to its potential, there would be no lectures from economists or policymakers about how to improve performance. This would be the fairy tale version (where dreams come true) of macroeconomic performance — the ideal hands-off scenario from Chapter 5.

No economist — hands-off or hands-on — believes this dream always comes true. But all recognize potential GDP as a key goal that economic performance should aim for in the short run.

A key long-run goal of economic performance is to increase the economy’s potential for producing products and services. This is the subject of economic growth — how to increase potential GDP over time in a fast and sustainable way. The best economic dreams are of a growing economy.
