Potential GDP

Potential GDP is a reference point for a well-functioning market economy, the outcome if Adam Smith’s invisible hand works perfectly. Potential GDP is real GDP when all inputs — labour, capital, land (and other resources), and ­entrepreneurial ability — are fully employed. When real GDP equals potential GDP, the outcome for the economy as a whole is the same as when smart choices of households and of businesses are coordinated in each separate market. Everyone is getting the most bang per buck for their choices. The whole is equal to the sum of the well-functioning parts.
