Underground Economy

Activities in the underground economy are purposefully hidden from government, either because they are illegal, or are legal but avoid taxes (which is also illegal). Imagine the response Statistics Canada would get from asking a drug dealer, “Would you please tell me the value of final products and services your business produced in 2014?”

Unreported legal productive activities include cash payments for home renovations or repairs (that avoid income tax for the renovator and HST for the consumer), and unreported tips earned by servers, taxi drivers, and other workers.

Economists estimate the size of the underground economy in Canada is between 5 and 15 percent of GDP.
