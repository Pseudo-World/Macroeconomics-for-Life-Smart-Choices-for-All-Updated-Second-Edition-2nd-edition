Acceptable Inflation

Most economists view a low inflation rate — under 3 percent per year — as not a serious problem, as long as the inflation rate is also predictable and steady from year to year. The Bank of Canada’s target range for the inflation rate is based on this view.

Since even a low inflation rate hurts people on fixed incomes, you may be wondering why economists don’t push for a zero percent inflation rate. That’s a good question, and the answer will come in the last section of this chapter on trade-offs between inflation and unemployment. Every choice, including the policy choice to aim for zero inflation, has an opportunity cost.
