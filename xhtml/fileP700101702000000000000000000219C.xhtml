The Lesson of Japanese Deflation

Japan suffered from a decade of deflation after its real estate bubble burst in 1991. Partly as a result of the destructive downward spiral of deflation, the Japanese economy suffered two decades of economic stagnation, almost no growth, and significant unemployment.

Lessons learned from the Japanese experience is one reason policymakers took strong action to try to avoid deflation during the Global Financial Crisis. Deflation is much worse than low inflation.
