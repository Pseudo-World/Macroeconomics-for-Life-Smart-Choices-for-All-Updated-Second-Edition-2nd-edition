7.1 Unemployment

  1. Who would be counted as unemployed?

    1. Sirena is a college student with no job.

    2. Miguel starts a new job in a week.

    3. Reetu stopped looking for work because she was unable to find a job.

    4. Rajinder is working part-time but wishes he was working full-time.

  2. If Salma loses her job and starts looking for work, the

    1. number of employed increases.

    2. labour force increases.

    3. labour force decreases.

    4. labour force participation rate remains unchanged.

  3. Suppose that there are 19 million people employed, 1 million unemployed, and 25 million people 15 years of age or older. Which statement is true?

    1. The labour force is 19 million.

    2. The labour force participation rate is 80 percent.

    3. The unemployment rate is 4 percent.

    4. The unemployment rate is 1 percent.

  4. The summer job market for post-secondary students starts in May. In May 2009, 59 000 fewer students were employed compared to a year earlier. These students’ labour force participation rate also fell over the year, from 75.2 percent to 68.6 percent. What is the correct interpretation of these results?

    1. More students are working.

    2. More students are in the labour force.

    3. Fewer students are looking for work.

    4. Fewer students are in the labour force.
