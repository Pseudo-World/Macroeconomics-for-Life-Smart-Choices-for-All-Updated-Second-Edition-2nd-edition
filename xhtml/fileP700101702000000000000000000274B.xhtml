Government Policy

Government policy changes affect aggregate demand. Fiscal policies involve tax and spending changes. Higher taxes are a negative demand shock. Consumers and businesses have less money to spend, decreasing aggregate demand. Decreases in government spending on products and services are also a negative demand shock. Tax cuts and more government spending are positive demand shocks, increasing aggregate demand.

Monetary policy by the Bank of Canada (coming in Chapter 10) affects interest rates and exchange rates, which in turn affect aggregate demand.
