Exchange Rates

Exchange rates among currencies change R.O.W. planned demand for Canadian exports and Canadiansâ€™ purchases of imports. When the Canadian dollar rises in value relative to the U.S. dollar or other currencies, Canadian exports become more expensive, so Americans and R.O.W. will buy fewer of them. Imports become cheaper, so Canadians buy more of them. When the Canadian dollar falls in value relative to other currencies, our exports become cheaper and R.O.W. will demand more of them. Imports become more expensive and we buy fewer of them. Exchange rates are the topic of Chapter 10.

A rise in the exchange rate is a negative demand shock, decreasing exports and increasing imports, decreasing Canadian aggregate demand. A fall in the exchange rate is a positive demand shock, increasing exports and decreasing imports, increasing Canadian aggregate demand.
