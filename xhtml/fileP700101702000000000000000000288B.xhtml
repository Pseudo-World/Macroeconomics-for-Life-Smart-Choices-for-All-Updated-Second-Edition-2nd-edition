Origins of Shocks and Business Cycles — “No” Camp

The “No — Markets Fail Often” camp believes that shocks to short-run aggregate supply and aggregate demand are largely internally generated as unintended byproducts of markets. Expectations, the role of money, and connections between different market economies in R.O.W. create shocks.

Note

For the “No — Markets Fail Often” camp, the origins of shocks are internal to the economy.

Note

Professors George Akerolf and Robert Shiller (both Nobel Prize winners) returned to Keynes’s ideas in their 2009 book, Animal Spirits, explaining business cycles and the Global Financial Crisis.

Because no one can foretell the future, investment plans are based on expectations, or informed guesses. The “No” camp sees investment decisions as based largely on a gut-level instinct to act, which Keynes called animal spirits — “a spontaneous urge to action rather than inaction.”
