What Changes the Demand for Money? Real GDP and Average Price Level

In macroeconomics, as in microeconomics, we distinguish a change in quantity demanded from a change in demand. Only a change in the price of money — the interest rate — changes the quantity demanded of money. A change in other factors changes the demand for money.

Figure 9.1 assumes that all factors, other than the interest rate, influencing the demand for money remain unchanged. Two factors that can change the macroeconomic demand for money are real GDP and the average price level. A change in either factor shifts the money demand curve.
