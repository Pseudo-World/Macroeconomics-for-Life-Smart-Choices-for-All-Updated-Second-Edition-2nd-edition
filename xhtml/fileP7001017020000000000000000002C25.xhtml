Banker to Chartered Banks

Chartered private banks take deposits from consumer households and businesses and make loans. While you might have demand deposits in a chequing account at Scotiabank, Scotiabank and all other chartered banks have their own demand deposits in accounts at the Bank of Canada. These Bank of Canada deposits allow the chartered banks to make payments to each other. When you use your Scotiabank debit card to buy jeans from a merchant who has an account at the Bank of Montreal, Scotiabank transfers the money to the Bank of Montreal through their accounts at the Bank of Canada.
