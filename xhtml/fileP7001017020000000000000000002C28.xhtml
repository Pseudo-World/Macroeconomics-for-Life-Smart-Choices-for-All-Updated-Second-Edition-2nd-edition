Lender of Last Resort

The Bank of Canada also is a lender of last resort to the financial system. When chartered banks are short of funds — for example, if borrowers go bankrupt and fail to repay loans owed to the bank — chartered banks can borrow from the Bank of Canada. This role helps maintain stability and liquidity in the financial system. During the Global Financial Crisis, the Bank of Canada, the U.S. Federal Reserve, and central banks around the world played crucial roles as lenders of last resort in preventing a breakdown of the financial system like that which occurred during the Great Depression.
