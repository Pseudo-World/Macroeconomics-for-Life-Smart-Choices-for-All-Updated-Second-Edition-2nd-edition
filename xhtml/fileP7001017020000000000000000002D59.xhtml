Money Does Not Directly Affect Aggregate Supply

There is no direct connection between changes in the money supply and changes in the aggregate supply of real GDP. Increases in aggregate supply come from increases in the quantity or quality of inputs, including technological change. Money is not an input into production like labour, capital (equipment), land and other natural resources, or entrepreneurship. And unlike technological change, changes in the money supply do not increase the quality of other inputs. Money does not directly increase (or decrease) aggregate supply or directly contribute to economic growth.
