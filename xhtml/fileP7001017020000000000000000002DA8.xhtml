Money Does Not Affect How Often Business Cycles Happen

Business cycles are triggered by demand or supply shocks that cause a mismatch between aggregate supply and demand. Say and his followers believe that most shocks to the economy come from forces outside the economy like scientific discoveries, technological change, and natural disasters. These aggregate supply shocks are the main source of business cycles for this camp. Adding money to the “real economy” of inputs, technology, and outputs doesn’t affect aggregate supply, and doesn’t create new shocks. Money, the “Yes — Markets Self-Adjust” camp states, does not significantly affect how often business cycles happen.
