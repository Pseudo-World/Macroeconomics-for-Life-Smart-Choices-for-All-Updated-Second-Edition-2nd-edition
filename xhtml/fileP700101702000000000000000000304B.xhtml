Supplying Canadian Dollars to Non-Canadians

Who supplies Canadian dollars to the worldwide foreign exchange market? Consider this scenario: You are heading south for some cross-border shopping in the United States. That makes you a Canadian who wants U.S. dollars. You must supply Canadian dollars in exchange. Your demand for U.S. dollars is also a supply of Canadian dollars. In the unique world of the foreign exchange market, the demand for one currency is the supply of another.

Note

The demand for one currency is the supply of another currency.

There are two main reasons why Canadians supply Canadian dollars on the foreign exchange market: to buy imports and assets from the rest of the world and to speculate on the future value of the Canadian dollar.
