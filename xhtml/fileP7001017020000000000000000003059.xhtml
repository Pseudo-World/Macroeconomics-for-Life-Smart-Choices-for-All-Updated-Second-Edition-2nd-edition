Demand for Imports and Foreign Assets

A Canadian who wants to buy imports must pay in the currency of the country supplying them. The dress in Buffalo that costs US$60 must be paid for with U.S. dollars. If you stay at Hotel California, or go to a Red Wings hockey game in Detroit, you are “importing” (using) U.S. hotel or entertainment services that must be paid for with U.S. dollars. To get U.S. dollars, you must exchange some of your Canadian dollars. Similarly, a Canadian investor who wants to buy U.S. assets must pay in U.S. dollars, which she buys using Canadian money. When you convert Canadian dollars into U.S. dollars, you are both supplying Canadian dollars and demanding U.S. dollars at the same time.

Note

The supply of Canadian dollars is Canadian demand for R.O.W. imports and assets, and for speculating on the future value of the Canadian dollar.
