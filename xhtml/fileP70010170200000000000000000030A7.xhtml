Equilibrium Exchange Rate

The equilibrium, or market-clearing, exchange rate is at the intersection of the demand and supply curves. In our example, that exchange rate is US$0.90. When a Canadian dollar sells for US$0.90, the quantity demanded of Canadian dollars (60 billion) equals the quantity supplied of Canadian dollars (60 billion).
