Interest Rate Differentials

There are usually differences in interest rates in different countries as well as differences in inflation rates. Economists call these differences differentials.

In the foreign exchange market, money flows almost instantly to where the rate of return — the interest rate on an investment — is highest. When investors buy bonds, stocks, businesses, or real estate, they search worldwide for the highest rate of return. If they can earn a 5 percent return in Canada, they will not accept a 3 percent return in the United States. The difference between the two interest rates is called the interest rate differential. When interest rates rise in Canada relative to other countries, it is an increase in the Canadian interest rate differential.
