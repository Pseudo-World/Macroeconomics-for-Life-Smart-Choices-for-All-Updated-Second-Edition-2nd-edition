Law of One Price

Once again, speculators play a key role. By buying low and selling high, speculators enforce the law of one price, which states that any time there are differences in the price of a product or service across markets, the actions of profit-seekers eliminate the differences and establish a single price.

Imagine this scenario: One carat diamonds are selling for US$1000 in South Africa but US$1500 in Canada. If you buy diamonds in South Africa, and sell them in Canada, you make US$500 per diamond. Buy low in South Africa â€” sell high in Canada. But as more people pursue these profits, demand for diamonds in South Africa increases, driving up prices there. The additional supply of diamonds in Canada drives prices down here. That US$500 price difference quickly disappears. As long as there is a difference in price, the profit-driven forces of demand and supply lead to a single price across both markets. The law of one price applies to all markets, including the foreign exchange market.
