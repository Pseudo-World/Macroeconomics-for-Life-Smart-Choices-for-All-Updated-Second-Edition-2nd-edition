Why International Payments Account Must Balance

All international transactions begin with an exchange in the foreign exchange market. When R.O.W. buys Canadian exports or invests in Canadian companies, they first buy enough Canadian dollars on the foreign exchange market to pay for those transactions. When Canadians buy imports or invest in companies from R.O.W., we first buy enough of each foreign currency to pay for those transactions. The demand for a different currency on the foreign exchange market is also a supply of your own currency.

Because demand for one currency is also the supply of another, the balance of payments accounts must add up to zero.
