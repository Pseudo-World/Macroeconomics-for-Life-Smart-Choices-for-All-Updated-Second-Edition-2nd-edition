Current Account Deficit and Financial Account Surplus

The current account deficit of $64 billion means that Canadians spent more on imports from R.O.W. than R.O.W. spent on Canadian exports. Where did Canadians get the extra $64 billion worth of foreign currency to pay for those imports? From the financial account surplus. The $55 billion financial account surplus means that R.O.W. invested more in Canada than Canadians invested in R.O.W. The R.O.W. effectively “loaned” Canadians the extra foreign currency necessary to finance our purchases of R.O.W. exports. The $9 billion statistical discrepancy completes the equality between the current account deficit and financial account surplus.
