Buying Bonds Increases the Price of Bonds

When the Bank of Canada buys large quantities of bonds on the bond market, the demand for bonds increases. This causes the price of bonds to rise. Bond prices and interest rates are inversely related. (To recall why, review section 9.3.) A rise in the price of bonds decreases the interest rate on bonds. By buying bonds, the Banks of Canada drives the interest rate down. In the example in Figure 11.3, the interest rate falls from 6 percent to 4 percent.

Note

When the Bank of Canada buys bonds, the increased demand for bonds raises bond prices and lowers interest rates.
