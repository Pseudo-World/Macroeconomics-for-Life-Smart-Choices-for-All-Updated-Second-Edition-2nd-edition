Selling Bonds Decreases the Price of Bonds

When the Bank of Canada sells large quantities of bonds on the bond market, the supply of bonds increases. This causes the price of bonds to fall. Bond prices and interest rates are inversely related. A fall in the price of bonds increases the interest rate on bonds. By selling bonds, the Bank of Canada drives the interest rate up. In the example in Figure 11.4, the interest rate rises from 6 percent to 8 percent.
