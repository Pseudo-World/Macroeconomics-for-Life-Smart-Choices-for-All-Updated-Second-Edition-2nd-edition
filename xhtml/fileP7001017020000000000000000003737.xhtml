Higher Interest Rates Are a Negative Aggregate Demand Shock

Higher interest rates increase the cost of borrowing, decreasing C and I, and decreasing aggregate demand. Higher interest rates are a negative demand shock and cause decreased real GDP, increased unemployment, and decreasing inflation or deflation (Chapter 8).

Note

Lower interest rates are a positive aggregate demand shock.

Higher interest rates are a negative aggregate demand shock.

We must add the effects of the international transmission mechanism to these domestic effects to get the net effect of monetary policy.
