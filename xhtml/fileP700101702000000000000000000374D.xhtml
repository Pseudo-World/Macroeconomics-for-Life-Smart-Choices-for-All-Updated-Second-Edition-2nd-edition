Depreciating Canadian Dollar Is a Positive Aggregate Demand Shock

The international effect of lower interest rates, just like the domestic effect, increases aggregate demand and accelerates the economy. Both the increase in exports (X) and the decrease in imports (IM) increase aggregate demand. Remember your mantra: Aggregate demand equals C+I+G+X−IM. This increase in net exports (X−IM) is a positive aggregate demand shock, causing increased real GDP, decreased unemployment, and rising inflation.
