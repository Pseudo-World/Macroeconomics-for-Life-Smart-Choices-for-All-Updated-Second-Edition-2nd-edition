Appreciating Canadian Dollar Is a Negative Aggregate Demand Shock

Higher interest rates increase the Canadian interest rate differential, causing the Canadian dollar to appreciate on the foreign exchange market. The higher exchange rate makes exports (X) more expensive for the R.O.W. and imports (IM) cheaper for Canadians. Net exports (Xâˆ’IM) decrease, decreasing aggregate demand. The appreciating Canadian dollar is a negative demand shock, causing decreased real GDP, increased unemployment, and decreasing inflation or deflation.
