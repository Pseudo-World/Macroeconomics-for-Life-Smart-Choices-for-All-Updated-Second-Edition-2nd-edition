Driving with an Unpredictable Transmission: Timing Is Everything

Even when all is going well, the timing of monetary policy — when to step on the accelerator or the brake — is difficult because central banks have to predict the impact up to two years in advance.

But when macroeconomic players resist borrowing, spending, and lending, and instead choose the security of money and savings, monetary policy becomes even more challenging. Balance sheet recessions cause transmission problems for monetary policy, making it harder to steer the economy toward recovery.

Facing falling asset values on their balance sheets, consumers, businesses, and banks make individually smart choices to resist borrowing, spending, and lending. But those individual smart choices did not add up to smart choices for the economy as a whole.
