Who’s Driving? Anchoring Inflation Expectations

  1. 11.5 Explain how inflation rate targeting by an independent central bank anchors expectations, helps market economies function, and gets economists to agree.

The Governor of the Bank of Canada has more power to influence economic outcomes than almost any other Canadian. In a democracy like Canada’s, it is unusual for an unelected official to be that powerful. The relationship between the Bank of Canada and our elected Parliament contains a classic Canadian compromise between government authority to act and accountability to voters. And speaking of compromises, the “Yes — Markets Self-Adjust, so Hands-Off” and “No — Markets Fail Often, so Hands-On” camps largely agree on the need for a central bank to prevent runaway inflation expectations and to help markets operate well.
