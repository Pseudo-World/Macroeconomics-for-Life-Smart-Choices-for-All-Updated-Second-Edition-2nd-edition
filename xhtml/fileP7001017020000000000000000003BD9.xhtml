Encourage Research and Development

Most productivity-improving technological change begins with research and development to improve ways of making existing products and services, and to develop new and better products and services. Government uses targeted government spending and tax incentives as fiscal policy to encourage research and development. The government funds the National Science and Engineering Research Council (NSERC), which subsidizes basic scientific research in universities. The government also provides tax incentives to businesses to encourage research and development.

Note

Government spending and tax incentives for research and development promote economic growth.
