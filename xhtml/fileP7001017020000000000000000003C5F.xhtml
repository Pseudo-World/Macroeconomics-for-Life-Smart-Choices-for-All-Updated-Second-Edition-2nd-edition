Government Spending

Government spends on purchases of products and services, transfer payments to businesses and individuals (like Employment Insurance benefits), and interest payments on the national debt (like your interest payments on a credit card balance). Figure 12.9 shows that we pay 8 percent of our taxes on interest payments (public debt charges) on the accumulated government debt. The largest expenditure is on direct program spending, which includes health care.
