Loaded Words

The words used in arguments about the proper role for government often reveal the speaker’s point of view.

For those supporting a hands-off role, the word government is often paired with words like intervene, interfere, and mistake. For example, “the government should not intervene . . . ,” “that is government interference . . . ,” or “it is a mistake for government to . . . .” These words show the speaker believes that government has no legitimate business in taking action, and if government does act, it usually gets it wrong.

For those supporting a hands-on role, the word government is often paired with words like act, participate, and responsibility. For example, “the government needs to act to . . . ,” “this initiative requires government participation,” or “the government has a responsibility to . . . .”

Look for these words when listening to a politician’s arguments. They are clues to the politician’s views on the proper role of government.
