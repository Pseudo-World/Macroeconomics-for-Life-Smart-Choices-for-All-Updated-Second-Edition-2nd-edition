Positive Statements

Here are some examples of positive statements in this chapter:

  • Tax incentives stimulate saving, increase the quantity of capital, and promote economic growth.

  • Tax cuts increase government tax revenues.

  • The national debt does not have to be repaid, only refinanced.

  • Automatic stabilizers create government budget deficits when the economy goes into a recession.

I am not claiming that any of these statements is actually true or false. But you can evaluate a positive statement as true or false by checking the facts. Some of the statements are true, and some are false. Most economists evaluate the claim by supply-siders that “tax cuts increase government tax revenues” as false because the facts show that when taxes were cut, revenues decreased, not increased. But the claim is still a positive statement — capable of being evaluated as true or false by checking the facts.
