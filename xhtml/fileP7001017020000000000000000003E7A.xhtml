Hands-Off or Hands-On? Your Choice

Arguments over fiscal policy are often the main event for fights between the “Yes — Markets Self-Adjust” camp so hands-off, and the “No — Markets Fail Often” camp so hands-on. Disagreement over taxes versus government spending, and over deficits, surpluses, and the national debt are often heated. But the arguments often mix politics and economics in misleading ways. Sort out the positive from the normative statements, and the economic arguments from the political arguments, so that you can make informed choices as a ­citizen about hands-off and hands-on roles for government fiscal policy and your own future.
