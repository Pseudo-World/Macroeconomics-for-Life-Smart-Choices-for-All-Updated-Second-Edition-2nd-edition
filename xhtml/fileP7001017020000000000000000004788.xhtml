True/False

  1. 1. False. Demand is a stronger word, meaning willing and able to pay.

  2. 2. True.

  3. 3. False. Also depends on your time and effort.

  4. 4. True. Marginal means additional.

  5. 5. True. Flat fee is not an additional cost.

  6. 6. False. Marginal benefit equals average benefit only in special circumstances. For example, if a basketball player with a shooting percentage of 50 percent s­uccessfully makes one out of her next two shots, then the additional points she adds are equal to the amount of points she usually (on average) adds.

  7. 7. True. Willingness to pay changes with circumstances.

  8. 8. False. Quantity demanded is a much more limited term than demand. Only a change in price changes quantity demanded. A change in any other influence on consumer choice changes demand.

  9. 9. True. Only a change in price changes quantity demanded.

  10. 10. True. Add up all individual demands at any price to get market demand.

  11. 11. False. Slope downward to the right.

  12. 12. True. For any quantity, maximum willing to pay is now less.

  13. 13. False, for normal goods. A decrease in income d­ecreases demand. But true, for inferior goods.

  14. 14. False. As the holidays get nearer, people’s willingness and ability to pay for certain products or services increases for any given price. Therefore, an increase in demand drives the rising prices.

  15. 15. False. Statement is true for normal goods, false for inferior goods.
