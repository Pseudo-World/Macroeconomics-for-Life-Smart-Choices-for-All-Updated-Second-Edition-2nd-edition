True/False

  1. 1. True. Temporary layoffs count as unemployed.

  2. 2. False. Unemployed last month but not in the labour force this month because he gave up searching.

  3. 3. True. V. J. went from unemployed to not in the labour force.

  4. 4. False. Number of employed stayed the same and number of unemployed decreased, so unemployment rate decreased. Last month it was 50% = unemployed ÷ labour force × 100 = 2 ÷ (2 + 2) × 100. This month it was 33% = 1 ÷ (1 + 2) × 100.

  5. 5. True. C. J. and V. J. would then count as unemployed.

  6. 6. True. Due to business cycle.

  7. 7. False. B. J. is cyclically unemployed, so no full e­mployment.

  8. 8. False. There was no cyclical unemployment if A. J. and B. J. are employed, C. J. and V. J. are structurally unemployed, and D. J. is not in the labour force.

  9. 9. False. Stagflation, not stagnation, if prices are rising and output is decreasing.

  10. 10. False. It would overestimate A. J.’s true cost of living increase since A. J. does not consume the products and services rising in price so does not have an increase in his cost of living.

  11. 11. False. Real interest rate is zero equals nominal interest rate minus the inflation rate.

  12. 12. True. Increase in quantity of money causes equal percentage increase in inflation rate.

  13. 13. False. Velocity = 2 because M × V = 100, and M = 50.

  14. 14. True. Inverse relation between unemployment rate and inflation rate.

  15. 15. False. With cost-push inflation, inflation and unemployment change in the same direction.
