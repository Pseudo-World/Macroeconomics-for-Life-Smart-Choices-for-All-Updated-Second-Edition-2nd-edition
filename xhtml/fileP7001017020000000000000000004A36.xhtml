True/False

  1. 1. True. Saving is a leakage.

  2. 2. False. “No — Markets Fail Often” camp favours government spending to accelerate the economy.

  3. 3. False. To slow the economy, “Yes — Markets Self-Adjust” camp favours spending reductions; “No — Markets Fail Often” camp favours tax increases.

  4. 4. True. Government purchases have a multiplied effect on aggregate demand and real GDP.

  5. 5. False. Empirical evidence shows tax revenues decrease with cuts in the tax rate.

  6. 6. False. Those disagreeing with supply-sider arguments refer to “voodoo economics.” Supply-siders would not want your hug.

  7. 7. False. Keynes, the original hands-on economist, believes short-run adjustment costs of savings outweigh long-run benefits for economic growth.

  8. 8. True. Knowledge has positive externalities for growth.

  9. 9. True. Tax cuts decrease government revenues.

  10. 10. True. Automatic stabilizers support spending and decrease taxes.

  11. 11. False. Automatic stabilizers drop transfers when the economy’s hot and decrease taxes or increase transfers when it’s not.

  12. 12. False. Debt is a stock and sometimes a smart choice. Deficits are a flow.

  13. 13. False. Ricardo and Barro agree that government spending financed by borrowing (debt) has no impact on the economy.

  14. 14. True. Normative because there are different judgements about what “better” means.

  15. 15. False. Founding principles for Canada are peace, order, and good government; for the United States they are life, liberty, and the pursuit of happiness.
